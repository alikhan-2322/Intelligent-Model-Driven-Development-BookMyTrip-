Certainly! Below is a simple data model for the BookMyTrip application, including key entities and their fields:

- **Trip**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the trip.
  - **destination**: Location where the trip is headed.
  - **startDate**: Date when the trip begins.
  - **endDate**: Date when the trip ends.
  - **price**: Cost of the trip.

- **Booking**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the booking.
  - **userId**: Identifier for the user who made the booking.
  - **tripId**: Identifier for the trip that is booked.
  - **status**: Current status of the booking (e.g., confirmed, canceled).

- **User**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the user.
  - **name**: Full name of the user.
  - **email**: Email address of the user.

- **Payment**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the payment transaction.
  - **bookingId**: Identifier for the associated booking.
  - **amount**: Amount paid for the booking.
  - **paymentDate**: Date when the payment was made.
  - **paymentMethod**: Method used for payment (e.g., credit card, PayPal).

- **Review**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the review.
  - **tripId**: Identifier for the trip being reviewed.
  - **userId**: Identifier for the user who wrote the review.
  - **rating**: Rating given to the trip (e.g., 1 to 5 stars).
  - **comment**: Textual feedback provided by the user.

These entities and fields provide a foundational structure for managing trips, bookings, users, payments, and reviews within the BookMyTrip application.
