Certainly! Here's a simple data model for the BookMyTrip application, featuring key entities and their fields:

- **Trip**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the trip.
  - **destination**: Location where the trip is going.
  - **startDate**: Date when the trip starts.
  - **endDate**: Date when the trip ends.
  - **price**: Cost of the trip.
  - **availableSeats**: Number of seats available for booking.

- **Booking**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the booking.
  - **userId**: Identifier for the user who made the booking.
  - **tripId**: Identifier for the trip being booked.
  - **status**: Current status of the booking (e.g., confirmed, canceled).
  - **bookingDate**: Date when the booking was made.

- **User**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the user.
  - **name**: Full name of the user.
  - **email**: Email address of the user.
  - **phoneNumber**: Contact number of the user.
  - **membershipStatus**: Indicates membership level/status (e.g., regular, premium).

- **Payment**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the payment.
  - **bookingId**: Identifier for the booking associated with this payment.
  - **amount**: Total amount paid.
  - **paymentDate**: Date when the payment was processed.
  - **paymentMethod**: Method of payment used (e.g., credit card, PayPal).

- **Review**
  - **id**: Unique identifier for the review.
  - **userId**: Identifier of the user who wrote the review.
  - **tripId**: Identifier of the trip being reviewed.
  - **rating**: Numeric rating given to the trip.
  - **comments**: Text comments from the user about the trip.
  - **reviewDate**: Date when the review was submitted.

These entities enable core functionalities such as searching for trips, booking and canceling trips, managing user information, processing payments, and collecting user feedback through reviews.
